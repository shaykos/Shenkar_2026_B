The Last Light

Sarah climbed the lighthouse stairs for the final time. Fifty years of guiding ships through storms had worn the stone smooth beneath her hands.

At the top, she gazed across the dark ocean. Her successor would arrive tomorrow—a young woman with technology, GPS, and algorithms. The lighthouse would become a tourist attraction.

She lit the great lamp one last time. Its beam swept across the water, steady and true, just as it always had. Somewhere out there, a fishing vessel saw it and changed course to safety.

Sarah smiled. Some things couldn't be replaced by machines.

Tomorrow, the light would pass to another. But tonight, it was still hers.
