Little Red Riding Hood

Once upon a time, a young girl with a bright red cloak walked through the forest to visit her grandmother. The villagers called her Red Riding Hood.

As she walked the winding path, a wolf approached her. "Good morning, child. Where are you headed?" it asked.

"To my grandmother's house with this basket of bread and jam," Red Riding Hood replied.

The cunning wolf nodded and suggested a shortcut through the trees. Red Riding Hood agreed, but the wolf raced ahead to the grandmother's cottage.

When Red Riding Hood arrived, she noticed something was wrong. "Grandmother, what big eyes you have," she said cautiously.

"All the better to see you with," the wolf growled.

Red Riding Hood screamed. A woodsman working nearby heard her cries, burst through the door, and chased the wolf away. He freed the grandmother from where she was hiding.

Red Riding Hood learned never to speak to strangers again. She and her grandmother lived safely ever after.
