The Last Library

Sarah stepped into the ancient library, dust motes dancing in the fading sunlight. Shelves stretched endlessly, holding stories from centuries past.

She ran her fingers along worn spines, each one a doorway to another world. The librarian, an elderly man with kind eyes, looked up from his desk.

"First time?" he asked.

"Yes," Sarah whispered. "I've spent my whole life with screens. I wanted to remember what it felt like to hold a real book."

He smiled and handed her a leather-bound volume. "Start here. Let yourself get lost."

As she opened the book, the library seemed to glow warmer. Outside, the city buzzed with digital noise. But here, in this sanctuary of paper and ink, time moved differently—slower, kinder, more real.

Sarah sat down and began to read, finally understanding why some places could never be replaced by technology.

THE END
